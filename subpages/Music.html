Music makes our lives better

People are constantly evolving, and so is technology. When CDs started to come in, so did music videos and excessive vibes. 80’s were colorful and diverse, and believe it or not, this decade made up for a golden era in pop, rock and hip hop music.

Pop music

Madonna was one of the first female artists to shine so bright in the lights of the stage. Sometimes, we even associate her with this decade because of her success back in the days. She revolutionized dance-pop music, coming out with her first album in early 80’s, including the memorable hit songs from “Holiday” to “Borderline”. She, of course, continued to come out with songs such as “Like a virgin”, “Open your heart” etc.

We cannot forget about the king of pop himself, Michael Jackson. After releasing “Off the wall” at the end of 1970’s, he came up with the album “Thriller” (1982) in collaboration with Quincy Jones, which became the best-selling album of all times, so it deffinetly left a mark.

The rise of Prince also started around 1980’s. This is the time when he started to have many successful hits in Billboard charts. His most commercial period started in 1982.

Rock music

New wave might be the most recognizable rock subgenre of the 80’s.

Billy Idol, just left Generation X at the time, and started his solo career, moving to New York, coming out with his debut solo album “Billy Idol”. Going solo led him to international recognition. In 1983, he dropped “Rebel Yell” which soon became a major commercial success.

The Go-Go’s had their career peak during 80’s. They are a female American new wave band. They signed up with I.R.S Records in April 1981 and dropped hit album “Beauty and the Beat”.

In 1983 Bon Jovi was formed but it only achieved global recognition in 1986, with their third album “Slippery when wet”, which is Bon Jovi’s best-selling album.

Another memorable mention is Guns n’ Roses, who released its debut studio album “Appetite for Destruction” around 1987 and became the best-selling album of all time, including hit songs “Welcome to the Jungle”, :Sweet child o’mine and “Paradise City”

Hip-hop music

Golden age of hip hop started in the mid/late 1980’s, because around those times it attained popularity. This genre originated from African’s Americans lifestyle.

The 80’s was the time of the crack cocaine epidemic, and was widely associated with black people, selling and taking drugs, often a topic of hip hop music lyrics.
